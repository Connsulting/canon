## Triage Notes
- Credit-card-like numeric regex hits in `.canon/ledger/*.json` were monotonic sequence metadata and pseudo-version suffixes, not cardholder data.
- Logging/error API inventory produced no high-confidence instances of PII field interpolation.
- No `.env*` files or plaintext credential assignments were present in tracked files.

## Findings
- file: `.gitignore`
- line: `1-9` (pre-remediation baseline)
- category: `gitignore-gap`
- severity: `high`
- detail: Baseline ignore policy lacked patterns for `.env*`, key material (`*.pem`, `*.key`), and credential naming (`credentials.*`, `secrets.*`), increasing risk of accidental commits containing secrets or user-data-bearing dumps.
- recommendation: Add explicit ignore rules for local secrets and common user-data dump artifacts (scoped patterns for dump/export/backup SQL/CSV/XLSX), and verify with `git check-ignore` plus tracked-file cross-checks.

## Remediation Applied
- Updated `.gitignore` with:
  - `.env`, `.env.*`, `*.pem`, `*.key`, `credentials.*`, `secrets.*`
  - scoped dump patterns under `dumps/**`, `exports/**`, `backups/**`
  - filename patterns: `*.dump.sql`, `*.backup.sql`, `*.export.csv`, `*.users.csv`, `*.pii.csv`
